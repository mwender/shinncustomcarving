Meet Bill

Telling stories in wood and other mediums

Bill Shinn takes great joy in surfacing designs, scenes, and faces with his carving tools. From gun stocks, to walking sticks, to spoons, forks, and more, Bill applies his skill, talent, and time to every project he undertakes.

Originally from West Virginia, Bill calls East Tennessee home. As an undergraduate at Concord University, he majored in physical education and art. Then he spent a year as a principal and a teacher at West Virginia elementary school. Next he spent eight years teaching art and coaching soccer and wrestling in a middle school. Finally, in 1979, Bill moved to Knoxville where he worked in middle and high schools through 2014. Now as a retired school teacher, he enjoys realizing his client’s designs in wood and other mediums.
